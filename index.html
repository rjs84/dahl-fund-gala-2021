About The Gala

"Heroes Are Not Forgotten"

The Heroes of Flight 93 Gala is an annual charity fundraising event held by the Captain Jason Dahl Scholarship Fund, which honors the memory of those lost in the September 11th attacks by sponsoring future aviators with scholarships. Join us for an evening of remembrance following the 20th Anniversary of September 11, 2001 with keynote speaker Heather Penney and a silent auction.

Keynote Speaker

Learn More About Heather Penney

Heather "Lucky" Penney

United States Air Force Pilot and Senior Resident Fellow for the Mitchell Institute for Aerospace Studies at the Air Force Association

Most widely recognized for her service on September 11, Heather “Lucky” Penney was part of the first wave of women who went directly into fighters from pilot training. Born on an Air Force base in Arizona, Heather Penney grew up around the military. Her father, retired Lt. Col. John Penney, flew jets, too. From age 4, she told her mother she wanted to be a fighter pilot, along with a ballerina, a butterfly, and a volcanologist. She fell in love, too, with jets and the camaraderie of the Air Force community.It wasn't until she got to Purdue University as an undergraduate in 1992 that she discovered women weren't allowed to be fighter pilots.

While she was still in college, Congress opened up combat aviation to women. In graduate school, she applied and got in. She was the only woman in her class.Her squadron gave her the name "Lucky" when she first arrived, as in "lucky Penney" She loved flying jets, riding at 500 knots, 30,000 feet up, totally focused on the task.

She was deployed to Iraq in 2003 and 2006, and then took a job at Lockheed Martin, the global security and aerospace company. She became a traditional reservist, which allowed Penney, then a divorced single mother, more time to raise her daughters, now 12 and 14. (She has since remarried.). Penney still flies for enjoyment and has her vintage planes, a Cessna 170 A and a Stearman PT13.

Learn More About the Captain Jason Dahl Scholarship Fund

Captain Jason Dahl Scholarship Fund

dahlfund.org

Founded in 2002 by Sandy Dahl, the Captain Jason Dahl Scholarship Fund is a charitable 501(c)3 that has raised over $347,000 in college scholarships for aspiring aviators. It originally awarded two scholarships per year to students at Metropolitan State University of Denver and San Jose State University (Captain Dahl's Alma Mater). In 2011, the Scholarship Fund was opened up to students in any accredited 4-year program, and it has now awarded 178 scholarships. Last year, $40,000 in scholarships were awarded to aviation students at 9 different universities.

The Dahl Fund is made strong by a legion of dedicated unpaid sponsors and volunteers, who make it possible to allocate nearly 100% of donations directly to scholarships. We seek to become the premier aviation scholarship in the country.

The Dahl Fund also sponsors an annual primary school program called "Heartland Heroes", which is a valuable learning tool for 5th graders, who learn about the heroics of Captain Jason Dahl and participate in an essay competition about heroes in their own lives. Currently sixteen Ken-Caryl area elementary schools participate every year.


The LeRoy W. Homer Foundation

leroywhomerjr.org

The LeRoy W. Homer Foundation was also founded in 2002 with the purpose of encouraging young adults aspiring to become professional pilots and increase awareness in the field of aviation. They have awarded 25 scholarships to students in aviation-related programs, all of whom now have a professional pilot's license.
